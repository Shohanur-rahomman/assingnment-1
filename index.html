The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy ensure sustainability, reduce emissions, promote energy independence, create green jobs, and drive renewable innovation..

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus, born on June 28, 1940, in Chittagong, Bangladesh, is an economist and social entrepreneur best known as the founder of Grameen Bank. He pioneered the concept of microcredit, providing small loans to impoverished individuals, especially women, to help them start businesses and break the cycle of poverty. Yunus’s innovative work earned him and Grameen Bank the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006. He remains a global advocate for poverty alleviation, social business, and sustainable development.
